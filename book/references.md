# References and further reading

These primary sources are not prerequisites. They are the next layer for
readers who want the original algorithms and experimental context.

- Ba, Kiros, and Hinton. *Layer Normalization* (2016).
  <https://arxiv.org/abs/1607.06450>
- Ho, Jain, and Abbeel. *Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models* (2020).
  <https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11239>
- Kingma and Ba. *Adam: A Method for Stochastic Optimization* (2014).
  <https://arxiv.org/abs/1412.6980>
- Loshchilov and Hutter. *Decoupled Weight Decay Regularization* (2017).
  <https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.05101>
- Mnih et al. *Human-level control through deep reinforcement learning*
  (2015). <https://doi.org/10.1038/nature14236>
- Radford et al. *Language Models are Unsupervised Multitask Learners* (2019).
  <https://cdn.openai.com/better-language-models/language_models_are_unsupervised_multitask_learners.pdf>
- Ronneberger, Fischer, and Brox. *U-Net: Convolutional Networks for Biomedical
  Image Segmentation* (2015). <https://arxiv.org/abs/1505.04597>
- Schulman et al. *Proximal Policy Optimization Algorithms* (2017).
  <https://arxiv.org/abs/1707.06347>
- Sennrich, Haddow, and Birch. *Neural Machine Translation of Rare Words with
  Subword Units* (2015). <https://arxiv.org/abs/1508.07909>
- Sutton and Barto. *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction*, second edition
  (2018). <http://incompleteideas.net/book/the-book-2nd.html>
- Vaswani et al. *Attention Is All You Need* (2017).
  <https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762>
- Williams. *Simple Statistical Gradient-Following Algorithms for
  Connectionist Reinforcement Learning* (1992).
  <https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00992696>
